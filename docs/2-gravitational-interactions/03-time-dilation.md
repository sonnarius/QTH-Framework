**Gravitational Time Dilation in Quantum Toroidal Harmonics (QTH)**

### 1. Introduction

In the QTH framework, time is not an independent background parameter. It emerges directly from the oscillation and tumbling rates of Photonic Toroids, which are governed by the local vacuum permeability **μ_{0}** and permittivity **ε_{0}**.

Because Photonic Toroids modify the local vacuum permittivity (creating δε > 0), they also affect the rate at which clocks run — a phenomenon known as **gravitational time dilation**.

### 2. Core Mechanism

The same permittivity gradient responsible for gravity also slows the internal dynamics of Photonic Toroids:

- Higher local ε → slower oscillation and tumbling frequencies inside the toroids.
- This is equivalent to gravitational time dilation: atomic transitions, radioactive decay, and the internal cycles of the toroids themselves run slower in regions of stronger gravitational potential.

In the Robinson metric, the redshift **z** directly quantifies this effect. The proper time interval dτ is related to coordinate time dt by:

**dτ = dt / (1 + z)**

### 3. Time Dilation in the Early Universe

In the hot, dense early universe, the number density of Photonic Toroids was orders of magnitude higher than today.

The cosmic **scale factor a** describes the relative size of the universe compared to today (**a = 1 today**, **a → 0** as we go back in time). Because the density of Photonic Toroids (and therefore the local permittivity shift δε) scales proportionally to the inverse cube of the scale factor:

** δε ∝ 1/a^{3} **

This resulted in:
- Much larger global δε everywhere in the cosmos.
- A significantly deeper average gravitational potential across the entire early universe.
- Strong **global gravitational time dilation**: clocks ran much slower compared with the reference frame of the present-day universe.

From our current perspective, the first few seconds after the initial condensation of Photonic Toroids corresponded to a much longer period of **proper time** inside the early plasma.

### 4. Connection to Matter-Antimatter Asymmetry

This extended proper time was essential for the chiral twist **Ω_χ** to establish the observed baryon asymmetry (η_b ≈ 6 × 10^{-10}).

The tiny handedness bias had significantly more time to act coherently before the universe cooled and the asymmetry became frozen. Without this early global time dilation, the chiral effect would have been too weak to produce the observed matter excess.

### 5. Comparison with Standard Cosmology

In conventional Big Bang cosmology, gravitational time dilation is usually considered only locally (near compact objects). In QTH, there is an additional **global** time dilation effect caused by the much higher average density of Photonic Toroids throughout the early universe.

This global effect is a natural consequence of the same permittivity mechanism that produces gravity at all scales.

### 6. Implications

- The early universe had substantially more proper time available for key processes (chiral bias, establishment of charge neutrality, initial structure formation).
- As the universe expanded and δε diluted (∝ 1/a^{3}), global time dilation gradually decreased.
- Clocks smoothly transitioned from the highly dilated early phase to the normal time flow we experience today.
