**Scaling with Cosmic Expansion in Quantum Toroidal Harmonics (QTH)**

### 1. Introduction

In the QTH framework, two of the most important quantities — the global chiral twist **Ω_χ** and the effective gravitational strength (via permittivity shifts δϵ) — are not constant. They evolve systematically as the universe expands.

This evolution is not arbitrary. It follows a simple and physically motivated scaling law that connects the early universe to the observations we make today.

### 2. The Scaling Law

Both the chiral twist and the permittivity shift scale proportionally to the baryon number density:

**Ω_χ ∝ 1/a³**  
**δϵ / ε₀ ∝ 1/a³**

where **a** is the cosmic **scale factor** (a = 1 today, and a → 0 as we go back in time toward the early universe).

This 1/a³ dependence arises naturally because the number density of Photonic Toroids (protons and neutrons) dilutes exactly as 1/a³ when the universe expands.

### 3. Physical Origin of the Scaling

The chiral twist **Ω_χ** is sustained by the presence of matter (Photonic Toroids). As the average distance between toroids increases with expansion, the collective chiral bias weakens proportionally to the density.

Similarly, the local permittivity shift δϵ is generated by the high-frequency electromagnetic oscillations inside Photonic Toroids. Fewer toroids per unit volume means smaller permittivity gradients, and therefore weaker emergent gravity.

### 5. Why This Scaling Matters

This single, simple scaling law explains why the same underlying mechanism (the chiral twist) can be strong enough in the early universe to produce the observed baryon asymmetry (η_b ≈ 6 × 10^{-10}) and galaxy spin asymmetry, yet weak enough today to produce only the tiny measured cosmic birefringence.

It provides a natural, parameter-free connection between sub-quantum structure and cosmological observations.

### 6. Early-Universe Cutoff and Baseline Constants

The 1/a³ scaling law for Ω_χ and δϵ is only applied after the first stable Photonic Toroids have condensed. At even earlier times (higher energies), the vacuum is in a different regime where:

- Stable toroidal structures have not yet formed.
- The concept of a perturbing δϵ becomes meaningless.
- Photons themselves have not yet emerged as free propagating modes.
- Time and causality are not yet defined in the conventional sense.

In this pre-condensation “sea of energy,” c and α are understood as fundamental, scale-invariant properties of our universe. The measurable values of ε₀ and μ₀ today are close to their baseline values, and only the perturbations (δϵ) scale with density. This naturally avoids any divergence at a = 0.
