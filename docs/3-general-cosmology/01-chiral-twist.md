**The Global Chiral Twist in Quantum Toroidal Harmonics (QTH)**

### 1. Introduction

One of the most elegant and observationally supported concepts in the QTH framework is the existence of a **global chiral twist** in the electromagnetic vacuum.

This small background rotation provides a single unifying mechanism that links sub-quantum particle formation with large-scale cosmological observations.

### 2. Definition of the Chiral Twist (Ω_χ)

The chiral twist **Ω_χ** is a preferred handedness or very slow rotation in the underlying electromagnetic vacuum. It can be visualized as a gentle, universe-wide twisting of the E and B field orientations.

- Today this twist is extremely weak: **Ω_χ ≈ 8.6 × 10^{-21} rad/s**
- It was much stronger in the early universe and scales proportionally to the baryon density (**∝ 1/a³**).

### 3. Observational Evidence: Cosmic Birefringence

The clearest direct evidence for the chiral twist comes from **cosmic birefringence** — a small rotation of the polarization plane of CMB photons as they travel across cosmic distances.

Recent high-precision measurements from the Atacama Cosmology Telescope (ACT Data Release 6, 2025) give:

**β ≈ 0.215° ± 0.074°** (2.9σ)

When combined with earlier Planck and WMAP data, the significance reaches approximately 4.7σ.

This tiny rotation is precisely what we expect if photons experience a cumulative twist from a background chiral vacuum over 13.8 billion years.

### 4. Role in the Early Universe

Because the strength of the chiral twist scales as **∝ 1/a³**, it was enormously stronger during the hot, dense early universe (a → 0).

This stronger early twist had two major consequences:
- It preferentially stabilized **matter-type Photonic Toroids** over antimatter-type ones → explaining the observed baryon asymmetry (η_b ≈ 6 × 10^{-10}).
- It imprinted a preferred handedness on collapsing structures → contributing to the observed galaxy spin asymmetry (stronger at higher redshift).

### 5. Connection to Galaxy Spin Asymmetry

Multiple independent analyses (including 2025 JWST JADES data by Lior Shamir and earlier SDSS/HST studies) show that spiral galaxies exhibit a statistically significant preference for one rotation direction, with the asymmetry being stronger at earlier cosmic times.

In QTH, this is a natural consequence of the residual chiral twist during the epoch of galaxy formation.

### 6. Current Status

The global chiral twist is currently one of the strongest observational pillars of the QTH framework. The 2025 birefringence measurements provide direct evidence for its existence today, while galaxy spin data support its important role in the early universe.
